National Quality Infrastructure

The institutional context of trust in measurements and products

The National Quality Infrastructure connects metrology, standardisation, accreditation, conformity assessment and market surveillance. The project describes how information from these areas can form a coherent knowledge system.

The metrology system operates in a wider environment of institutions and processes, including the Central Office of Measures, the Polish Committee for Standardization, the Polish Centre for Accreditation and the public statistics system.

Metrology

Measurements

Ensuring measurement traceability and reliability of results.

Standardisation

Requirements

Standards and reference documents for products, services and processes.

Accreditation

Competence

Confirming the competence of bodies performing testing, calibration and certification.

Conformity assessment

Trust

Processes confirming compliance with requirements and supporting market surveillance.

Role of the information system

The planned information system should help users understand relations between institutions, documents, services, processes and requirements. Its value lies not only in collecting data, but also in describing context, accountability and ways of using information.

One of the objectives is to integrate the national metrology infrastructure more closely with the state information infrastructure, especially public statistics resources and methods.

User groups

  • Public administration and regulators.
  • Testing and calibration laboratories.
  • Industry and technical service providers.
  • Research and expert organisations.
  • Organisations supporting product quality and safety.
  • Institutions producing and using statistical information.

Importance for Polish Metrology

Metrology is one of the foundations of the National Quality Infrastructure. Its digital organisation can support public service design, analysis of economic needs, inter-institutional cooperation and trust in data used in decision-making processes.

The project supports development of a shared data ecosystem in which information on metrology, standardisation, accreditation and conformity assessment can be used to monitor public policies, evaluate outcomes and strengthen economic innovation.
