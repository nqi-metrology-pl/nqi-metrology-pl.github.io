About

An information system for metrology data, processes and services

The project responds to the need to organise distributed metrology information and prepare foundations for its responsible use within the National Quality Infrastructure, public statistics and evidence-based policies.

Main objective

The main objective is to develop a concept for a comprehensive information system supporting Polish Metrology, from the description of processes and data resources to recommendations on interoperability and the organisation of future digital services.

The system should help users find information, compare concepts, identify data sources and design coherent services for public institutions, laboratories, industry and research organisations.

The project is carried out by the University of Szczecin under the Polish Metrology II programme. It aims to create methodological foundations for a system supporting ongoing observation, monitoring and assessment of the impact of metrology on the economy and society.

Rationale

  • Distributed data and documents describing metrology services.
  • The growing role of digital certificates, metadata and measurement traceability.
  • The need for a shared conceptual model for National Quality Infrastructure institutions.
  • Requirements for secure and interoperable information exchange.
  • The importance of public statistics for regular measurement of metrology impact.
  • The growing role of European data governance principles and data spaces.

Basic information

The project is interdisciplinary and combines economics, data science, public statistics and engineering perspectives.

Applicant

University of Szczecin

A public university responsible for coordination and research delivery.

Period

2024-2025

The project funding period covers 21 months of work.

Disciplines

Interdisciplinary project

Economics and finance, automation, electronics, electrical engineering, space technologies and mechanical engineering.

Cooperation

Experts and public statistics

The project assumes cooperation with technical university experts and public statistics services.

Scope of work

The project covers analytical, informational and organisational layers, without building a production transaction system.

Stage 1

Information environment audit

Analysis of information needs, literature review, inventory and quality assessment of data sources.

Stage 2

System and survey design

Defining the information scope, data source integration concept and architecture of statistical information.

Stage 3

Handbook and recommendations

Preparing recommendations, public statistics survey proposals and assumptions for system organisation.

Dissemination

Reports and publications

Presenting results through publications, reports, seminars and publicly available materials.

Planned outputs

  • Specification of information and functional requirements.
  • Information model for Polish Metrology.
  • Data architecture and concept for integrating information sources.
  • Recommendations for National Quality Infrastructure stakeholders.
  • Proposals for new or extended public statistics surveys.
  • Handbook on organising information systems for metrology and quality infrastructure.

Partners and context

The project is embedded in the environment of the Central Office of Measures, the National Quality Infrastructure, technical universities and public statistics. Particular importance is given to cooperation with institutions that hold data, methodological knowledge and experience in research on science, technology, innovation and information and communication technologies.
