Metrology

Measurement quality as a foundation of trust in data

Metrology provides measurement traceability, comparability of results and reliability of information used in the economy, science, administration and health protection.

In the project, metrology is treated as a public good and development infrastructure: it supports innovation, industrial competitiveness, fair economic exchange, trade, regulation and evidence-based policies.

Role in the project

The information system for Polish Metrology should reflect relations between standards, services, documents, measurement data and users. This allows metrology information to be found, interpreted and used more effectively in decision-making processes.

An important task is to create foundations for monitoring the impact of metrology on the economy and society, including through better use of administrative and statistical data sources.

Digital metrology

  • Digital calibration certificates and measurement documents.
  • Unambiguous identifiers for services, organisations and resources.
  • Metadata describing uncertainty, measurement conditions and responsibility.
  • Linking data with requirements from standards and regulations.
  • Information needed for analyses, diagnoses and forecasts supporting metrology development.
